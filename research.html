Robot Learning & Reinforcement Learning

Advanced learning systems for robotics and autonomous decision-making

October 1, 2024 Mariano Phielipp

My research in robot learning focuses on developing efficient reinforcement learning algorithms that enable robots to learn complex tasks through interaction with their environment. This includes work on policy optimization, multi-task learning, and sample-efficient learning methods that can be applied to real-world robotic systems.

At Intel AI Labs, we're particularly interested in developing learning systems that can handle the challenges of real-world robotics, including uncertainty, partial observability, and the need for safe exploration. Our work spans both theoretical advances and practical applications in areas such as robotic manipulation, navigation, and human-robot interaction.

Key Publications:

  • "Moto: Offline pre-training to online fine-tuning for model-based robot learning" (CoRL 2023) - 17 citations
  • "Learning modular language-conditioned robot policies through attention" (Autonomous Robots 2023) - 12 citations
  • "Anymorph: Learning transferable polices by inferring agent morphology" (ICML 2022) - 33 citations

AI for Materials Science

Accelerating scientific discovery through artificial intelligence

September 20, 2024 Mariano Phielipp

Artificial intelligence is revolutionizing the field of material sciences by enabling researchers to discover and design new materials with unprecedented speed and accuracy. My work in this area focuses on developing AI systems that can predict material properties, optimize material synthesis processes, and accelerate the discovery of novel materials with specific desired characteristics.

We leverage machine learning techniques including deep learning, graph neural networks, and reinforcement learning to model complex material systems and predict their behavior. This includes work on crystal structure prediction, property prediction, and the design of materials for specific applications such as energy storage, catalysis, and electronic devices.

AI for Electronic Design Automation (EDA)

Revolutionizing chip design through artificial intelligence

August 10, 2024 Mariano Phielipp

Electronic Design Automation (EDA) is undergoing a transformation thanks to the introduction of AI, making processes more efficient and opening up new possibilities for chip design. My research focuses on developing AI systems that can automate and optimize various aspects of the chip design process, from floorplanning to circuit optimization.

We develop machine learning approaches for analog circuit modeling, floorplanning optimization, and design constraint satisfaction. This includes work on graph neural networks for circuit representation, reinforcement learning for design optimization, and collaborative conversational agents for physical circuit design.

Key Publications:

  • "FloorSet-a VLSI Floorplanning Dataset with Design Constraints of Real-World SOCs" (ICCAD 2024) - 4 citations
  • "PARSAC: Fast, Human-quality Floorplanning for Modern SoCs with Complex Design Constraints" (arXiv 2024) - 2 citations
  • "Pretraining graph neural networks for few-shot analog circuit modeling and design" (IEEE TCAD 2022) - 39 citations

Research Areas:

  • VLSI Floorplanning and Placement
  • Analog Circuit Modeling
  • Design Constraint Optimization
  • Graph Neural Networks for EDA
  • Conversational AI for Design
  • Few-Shot Learning for Circuits

Neural Networks & Optimization

Advancing deep learning architectures and optimization techniques

July 15, 2024 Mariano Phielipp

My research in neural networks and optimization focuses on developing more efficient, robust, and scalable deep learning systems. This includes work on neural network architecture design, optimization algorithms, and techniques for improving the performance and reliability of AI systems.

We explore various approaches including neuroevolution, multi-objective optimization, ensemble methods, and techniques for handling uncertainty in neural networks. This research has applications across multiple domains including computer vision, natural language processing, and robotics.
