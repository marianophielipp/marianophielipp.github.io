Robot Learning & Reinforcement Learning

Advanced learning systems for robotics and autonomous decision-making

October 1, 2024 Mariano Phielipp

My research in robot learning focuses on developing efficient reinforcement learning algorithms that enable robots to learn complex tasks through interaction with their environment. This includes work on policy optimization, multi-task learning, and sample-efficient learning methods that can be applied to real-world robotic systems.

At Intel AI Labs, we're particularly interested in developing learning systems that can handle the challenges of real-world robotics, including uncertainty, partial observability, and the need for safe exploration. Our work spans both theoretical advances and practical applications in areas such as robotic manipulation, navigation, and human-robot interaction.

Key Research Topics:

  • Deep Reinforcement Learning for Robotics
  • Multi-task and Transfer Learning
  • Safe Exploration and Risk-Aware Learning
  • Sample-Efficient Learning Algorithms
  • Human-Robot Collaboration

AI for Material Sciences

Accelerating scientific discovery through artificial intelligence

September 20, 2024 Mariano Phielipp

Artificial intelligence is revolutionizing the field of material sciences by enabling researchers to discover and design new materials with unprecedented speed and accuracy. My work in this area focuses on developing AI systems that can predict material properties, optimize material synthesis processes, and accelerate the discovery of novel materials with specific desired characteristics.

We leverage machine learning techniques including deep learning, graph neural networks, and reinforcement learning to model complex material systems and predict their behavior. This includes work on crystal structure prediction, property prediction, and the design of materials for specific applications such as energy storage, catalysis, and electronic devices.

Research Applications:

  • Material Property Prediction
  • Crystal Structure Design
  • Synthesis Process Optimization
  • High-Throughput Screening
  • Computational Materials Discovery

Computer Vision & Perception

Advanced visual understanding for AI systems

August 10, 2024 Mariano Phielipp

Computer vision research focuses on developing systems that can understand and interpret visual information from the world. My work spans multiple areas including object detection and recognition, scene understanding, visual reasoning, and the integration of vision with other modalities such as language and action.

We develop deep learning architectures and training methods that can handle the challenges of real-world vision tasks, including robustness to variations in lighting, viewpoint, and object appearance. This includes work on few-shot learning, domain adaptation, and the development of vision systems that can operate efficiently in resource-constrained environments.

Research Areas:

  • Object Detection and Recognition
  • Scene Understanding and Segmentation
  • Visual-Language Integration
  • Few-Shot and Zero-Shot Learning
  • Robust Vision Systems

Natural Language Processing

Language understanding and generation for AI systems

July 15, 2024 Mariano Phielipp

Natural language processing research focuses on developing systems that can understand, generate, and interact with human language. My work in this area includes language modeling, machine translation, question answering, and the development of conversational AI systems.

We explore both traditional NLP techniques and modern transformer-based approaches, with particular emphasis on developing systems that can handle multiple languages, domains, and tasks. This includes work on multilingual models, domain adaptation, and the integration of language understanding with other AI capabilities such as vision and reasoning.

Key Research Topics:

  • Language Modeling and Generation
  • Machine Translation
  • Question Answering and Reasoning
  • Multimodal Language Understanding
  • Conversational AI
