About Mariano Phielipp

Principal Engineer, Intel AI Labs | PhD in Computer Science

October 1, 2024 Mariano Phielipp

Professional Overview

I am a Principal Engineer at Intel AI Labs, where I lead research initiatives that partner with the external academic community to advance AI capabilities. With 18 years of experience at Intel, I have contributed to the evolution from software engineering to applied research and AI R&D. My work focuses on leveraging diverse data sources including natural language, perception, and action in robotics to develop innovative, efficient, goal-oriented policies.

Research Focus

My research spans multiple domains of artificial intelligence, with particular expertise in:

  • Deep Learning & Reinforcement Learning: Developing efficient and scalable learning systems for complex decision-making tasks
  • Robot Learning: Multi-task learning, human-robot interaction, and autonomous systems
  • AI for Materials Science: Crystal design, molecular discovery, and materials optimization using machine learning
  • Computer Vision: Object recognition, scene understanding, and visual perception
  • AI for EDA: Electronic design automation, circuit optimization, and chip design

Education & Background

PhD in Computer Science - Arizona State University
Master's in Computer Science - Arizona State University
Specialization in Graphics, Visualization, AI, Machine Learning, and Visual Perception

My academic background provided a strong foundation in computer science fundamentals, which I've applied throughout my career in developing cutting-edge AI systems and algorithms.

Career at Intel

Since joining Intel in 2006, I have held various roles that have allowed me to contribute to the company's technological advancement:

  • Software Engineering (2006-2012): Developed software solutions and tools for Intel's product development
  • Applied Research (2012-2018): Transitioned to research-focused roles, working on emerging technologies
  • AI R&D (2018-Present): Leading AI research initiatives at Intel AI Labs

Research Impact

My work has been published in top-tier conferences and journals including NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, AAAI, CoRL, and Autonomous Robots. I have collaborated with leading researchers in the field, including Pieter Abbeel, Chelsea Finn, and Heni Ben Amor. My research has contributed to advances in robot learning, AI for materials science, computer vision, and neural network optimization.

Recent notable publications include "Moto: Offline pre-training to online fine-tuning for model-based robot learning" (CoRL 2023), "Learning modular language-conditioned robot policies through attention" (Autonomous Robots 2023), and "Group SELFIES: a robust fragment-based molecular string representation" (Digital Discovery 2023).

Collaborations & Partnerships

At Intel AI Labs, I actively partner with academic institutions and research organizations to advance AI capabilities. Our team collaborates with universities and research labs worldwide to develop innovative solutions for real-world challenges. These partnerships have resulted in multiple patents, publications, and technological breakthroughs.

Professional Interests

I am passionate about advancing the field of artificial intelligence and its applications in robotics, materials science, and electronic design. I enjoy mentoring students and researchers, participating in academic conferences, and contributing to the broader AI community through publications, presentations, and collaborations.
