Principal Engineer at Humanoid

Leading Visual Language Action for Humanoid Robotics

2025 Mariano Phielipp

I'm excited to announce my new role as Principal Engineer at Humanoid, where I lead the Visual Language Action development for the company. This position focuses on advancing applied research and development in humanoid robotics, integrating AI-driven solutions to enhance robotic functionalities and improve human-robot interactions.

My work involves collaborating with cross-functional teams to design and implement machine learning algorithms, mentoring junior engineers and scientists, and fostering a culture of continuous learning and innovation in the field of humanoid robotics. I'm currently working on Visual Language Action (VLA) models and Human-Robot Interaction (HRI) projects.

Recent Publications & Research Impact

Latest work in AI, Robotics, and Machine Learning

2025 Mariano Phielipp

My research spans multiple domains of artificial intelligence, with recent focus on AI for Science, AI for EDA, and Robot Learning. Recent work includes foundational models in Language Conditioned Imitation Learning (NeurIPS Spotlight Paper), Goal Conditioned DRL research with Pieter Abbeel, and Model-Based DRL research with Chelsea Finn at Stanford.

Intel AI Labs Leadership

Principal Engineer in Deep Learning & AI Research

December 2024 Mariano Phielipp

From 2006 to 2024, I contributed to Intel's technological advancement across multiple roles. As Principal Engineer in Intel AI Labs (2020-2024), I led Deep Reinforcement Learning research and conducted applied research on Large Language Models and Reinforcement Learning for silicon manufacturing validation, achieving potential savings of millions of dollars per month.

My work included solving constrained combinatorial optimization problems in Electronic Design Automation (EDA), resulting in 30% speed improvement in floorplanning techniques, and collaborating with leading researchers including Pieter Abbeel, Chelsea Finn, Heni Ben Amor, and Ken Goldberg.
